# Grasp Patterns by Object Type

## Small Rigid Objects (Lego bricks, bottle caps, dice)
- Approach angle: top-down preferred
- Gripper width: 60–80% open before descent
- Close speed: slow
- Confirmation: object visually absent from table surface

## Cylindrical Objects (cups, pens, bottles)
- Approach angle: side grasp preferred (not top-down)
- Gripper width: match to estimated diameter
- Close speed: moderate; stop if resistance felt
- Note: top-down on a cylinder risks knocking it over

## Flat/Thin Objects (cards, sheets of paper, flat tiles)
- Approach angle: slight angle (15–20° from horizontal)
- Gripper width: barely open — just enough clearance
- Use edge grasp, not face grasp
- High failure rate on smooth surfaces — slide to edge first if possible

## Fragile/Deformable Objects (foam, fabric, soft toys)
- Use minimum gripper force
- Wide approach, slow close
- Do NOT apply standard close — stop at first contact indication

## Stacked Objects
- Always take from the top of the stack
- Confirm stack height via camera before approach
- Do not attempt to pull from middle of stack
